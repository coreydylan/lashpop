Pivot — Full-Screen Overlay

Stop fighting the grid

You're right — inline expansion forces the photo to stretch to match content height, which is why portraits end up ~900px tall fighting against portfolio photos that aren't. The fix is to stop expanding within the grid and open a real full-screen overlay where we control the proportions.

What changes

Today: Click card → inline expansion drops in below the row → height is driven by content → portrait + portfolio fight over a single column that's the wrong shape for both.

Proposed: Click card → modal overlay opens at ~95vw × 90vh → portrait gets a fixed-aspect panel, portfolio gets its own scroll region, content gets the breathing room it needs. The desktop modal already exists for mobile — we just use it everywhere.

Variant 1 Sticky Portrait · Scrolling Content

Sticky portrait, scrolling content + portfolio

Profile portrait pins to the left at its natural 3:4 aspect — never stretches, never crops weird. Right side scrolls independently through bio, credentials, quick facts, and the portfolio masonry. Identity always visible; content has room to breathe.

My recommendation

Variant 1 — Sticky Split

It's the lowest-effort change that completely solves the cropping problem. The portrait gets a fixed-shape panel (never stretches), the portfolio gets its own scroll region (any photo count fits), and identity + book button stay glued to the left so users never lose the artist they're browsing.

Critically, your mobile modal is already doing roughly this — portrait hero at top, scrollable content below. Switching desktop to full-screen overlay means one component handles both breakpoints, instead of the current "desktop inline expansion + mobile modal" split. Big maintenance win.

Variant 2 (Editorial Spread) is the long-term aspirational version — save it for when you build a dedicated /team/[slug] page that has its own URL and can be shared.
